About me

I am an undergraduate data science student and researcher at the University of Iowa (expected December, 2024). I am excited to use computer science and statistics to solve big problems confronting us in the 21st century. I am particularly interested in climate change and ecosystem health. My past research includes global Earth systems modeling at Arizona State University where we examined emissions reduction priorities. At the National Institutes of Environmental Health Sciences, I worked to evaluate a new flexible statistical method for toxicology data. Currently, I am working on a project at University of Iowa to develop methods to use remote sensing data to detect algal blooms in lakes. Outside of research, I enjoy hiking and traveling as well as Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. In the future, I hope to continue my growth as a computer scientist and statistician and apply my skills to new challenging problems.
